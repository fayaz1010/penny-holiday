House-sitting sounds too good to be true — until you realize people pay $129/yr just for the chance to live rent-free in someone's London flat for a month. The math is brutal: one 3-week sit in Paris saves $2,400+ in hotel costs. This guide breaks down exactly how the platforms work, how to build reviews fast, and which cities have the most sits.

What House-Sitting Actually Is

The deal: a homeowner goes traveling for 5 days to 6 months and needs someone to live in their place — water plants, collect mail, and most commonly look after pets (dogs, cats, the occasional chicken). You sleep in their bed, use their kitchen and pay zero rent. No money changes hands. Both sides save big: they avoid $40–$80/day kennel fees, you avoid $100–$300/night hotels.

How TrustedHousesitters Actually Works

Sign up, build a profile (photos, bio, references), get verified (police check optional but recommended). Browse sits filtered by date and country. Apply with a personalised message — generic copy-paste applications get ignored. Owner reviews applicants, video-calls 2–3 finalists, picks one. After the sit, both sides leave a public review. Build 3–5 five-star reviews and you'll start getting accepted for premium sits in London, Paris, Sydney within weeks.

Building Reviews From Zero

The hardest part is your first 2–3 sits — owners want proof. Three quick paths in: (1) Sit for a friend or family member with pets and ask for a written review you can post. (2) Apply for short, less-glamorous sits (3-day weekend in a small town) where competition is low. (3) Volunteer with local rescue groups and get a character reference. After your third real review, the doors open.

Money Tip: Apply within 24 hours of a sit being posted. Owners often pick from the first 5 applicants — being applicant 30 means you're already invisible.

What Hosts Actually Expect

Reliability above all. You stay in the home (no leaving the dog alone for 12 hours to go to a wedding). You handle minor pet needs — daily walks, feeding, medication. You leave the place cleaner than you found it. Most hosts also expect: keeping plants alive, collecting mail, basic security (lights on at night). What they don't expect: gardening, paying utilities, or doing major housework. Always read the full sit description before applying.

Top Sit-Rich Cities Right Now

Lesser-known goldmine: Lisbon and Berlin, where digital-nomad expats travel constantly and need sitters monthly.

Writing an Application That Gets Accepted

Address the host by name. Mention their pet by name. Mention something specific from their listing ("I love that you have a garden — I used to look after my grandmother's roses"). Include your relevant experience and one personal detail (you're a vet nurse, you grew up with cats). End with a sentence inviting a video call. Keep it under 200 words. This three-paragraph template alone increases acceptance rates dramatically.

Combining House-Sits Into a Full Travel Year

Experienced sitters chain sits together — finish a 3-week London sit, train to Paris for a 10-day sit, fly to Lisbon for two weeks. With 6–8 sits a year averaging 2 weeks each, you've covered 84+ days of accommodation for $0. At $100/night avg, that's $8,400 saved annually for one $129 membership.

Building Your First Sit Profile (The Bit Most People Get Wrong)

A bad profile is why most first-time sitters get rejected for 30+ applications before their first acceptance. A great profile gets you accepted within your first 5 applications. The difference is mostly preparation, not luck. Spend 90 minutes once on the elements below and you'll never struggle for sits again.

Photos: 6 Specific Shots You Need

Hosts skim profiles in under 30 seconds. Photos do 80% of the persuading. Upload, in order: (1) a clear smiling face shot in natural light, no sunglasses; (2) you with a dog or cat, ideally not your own — borrow a friend's; (3) you in a tidy home environment (couch, kitchen) showing you're a normal indoor human; (4) you traveling somewhere recognisable; (5) a wide outdoor shot showing you doing an activity (hiking, cycling, gardening); (6) a couple/family group photo if applicable. Avoid party shots, group selfies where your face is small, and anything blurry. iPhone-quality is fine — composition matters more than camera.

Bio: The 250-Word Formula That Works

Open with one sentence about who you are and why you sit ("I'm Sarah, a freelance copywriter who's been house-sitting for 3 years across the UK and Europe — I love the slow-travel rhythm and the chance to live in a real neighbourhood"). Follow with three short paragraphs: your pet experience (specific: "grew up with two Labradors, currently care for my sister's senior cat weekly"), your home-care approach ("I treat every home like my grandmother's — clean as I go, water plants on schedule, mail collected daily"), and what makes you reliable (full-time remote work, no kids, non-smoker, references available). Close with one warm sentence inviting a video call.

References From Zero

You don't need TrustedHousesitters reviews to start. Three workarounds: (1) Sit for a friend or family member with pets and ask them to write a written character reference — uploaded as a PDF to your profile. (2) Get a written reference from a previous landlord, employer or volunteer coordinator confirming reliability and trustworthiness. (3) Volunteer two weekends at a local animal rescue and ask the manager for a reference. Three written references plus a verified background check (the optional $40 add-on) makes you indistinguishable from a sitter with 5 reviews.

Money Tip: The optional $40 background check pays for itself within one application. Hosts filter by "verified" sitters and you'll appear in front of 4x more eyeballs. It's the single highest-ROI dollar in this whole process.

What to Actually Expect on a Sit

The Instagram version of house-sitting is sunlit kitchens, golden retrievers, and Parisian rooftop views. The reality is mostly that — but with daily logistics that nobody mentions until you're holding a senior dog's pill and a confused expression. Here's what an average sit actually involves.

Daily Duties By Pet Type

Dog sits are the most demanding: 2–3 walks daily (45 minutes total minimum), feeding twice, sometimes a midday potty break. Senior dogs may need medication on a strict schedule. Cat sits are gentler: feed twice, scoop the litter box daily, 15 minutes of play. Multi-cat homes can need 30–45 minutes of cleaning daily. Bird and reptile sits are minimal time but need precise environmental control. Farm sits with chickens, goats or horses can be 3–5 hours of physical work daily — these are usually clearly disclosed and often offer paid arrangements.

The Handover and Communication Pattern

Most hosts arrive home the day before you start (or you arrive 24 hours early) for a face-to-face handover. Expect a written "house manual" covering pet routines, vet contact, Wi-Fi, neighbour relations, and emergency procedures. During the sit, communication frequency varies wildly: some hosts want daily photo updates, others prefer weekly check-ins. Always ask their preference upfront. Expect to send 2–3 photos every 2–3 days — it's the single biggest driver of glowing reviews.

What's Typically Provided vs Expected

Provided: pet food, treats, vet account contacts, sometimes welcome groceries, Wi-Fi, full kitchen access, often a parking space. Expected: you cover your own food, transport, and any personal expenses. Utilities are always covered by the host. You should leave the home cleaner than you found it — fresh sheets stripped, dishwasher empty, fridge emptied of perishables, bins out. Plan to spend 2–3 hours on cleaning the morning of departure. The cleaner the handover, the more glowing your review.

Money Tip: Bring a small "house gift" ($10–$20 — local chocolates, coffee or wine) and leave a handwritten thank-you card on the kitchen counter. Hosts mention this in 90% of 5-star reviews. It costs almost nothing and unlocks future referrals to their friends with pets.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I have to love pets to house-sit?

You don't have to love them all, but 80% of sits involve at least one cat or dog. If you'd rather sit without pets, filter for "no pets" — there are hundreds globally, mostly plant-watering and security stays.

How long before a sit should I apply?

Most sits get filled 2–6 weeks before start date. Apply within 24 hours of posting for the best chance. Last-minute sits (under a week away) often have less competition.

Is TrustedHousesitters worth $129 a year?

If you do even one 7+ day sit, you'll save $700–$2,000 versus hotels. Most active sitters complete 4–8 sits a year. Yes, it's worth it.

Do I get paid to house-sit?

On TrustedHousesitters, no — it's a free exchange. Some private arrangements pay $30–$80/day, especially in the US. Search "paid house-sitting" Facebook groups for those.

Can couples or families house-sit?

Couples are common and often preferred (two people = better pet coverage). Families with young kids are harder — many hosts specify "no children." Always check the listing carefully.
